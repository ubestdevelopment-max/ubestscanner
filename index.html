ABC-XYZ Two-Dimensional Classification

Assign every product to one of 9 categories before replenishing

The Two Axes
ABC — Sales Volume
A = top 70% · B = 70–90% · C = bottom 10%
XYZ — Demand Stability
X = stable (CV < 0.5) · Y = fluctuating (CV < 1) · Z = volatile (CV ≥ 1)
Result
9 product classes, each with its own replenishment parameters
X Stable Y Fluctuating Z Volatile
A High Auto-replenishMonitor closelyHigh-freq small batch
B Mid StandardWeekly reviewCautious
C Low Minimal stockOn-demand⚠️ Eliminate candidate
📋 Example — Classifying "Oat Milk 1L"
1
Sales rank check
Top 65% of all SKUs by volume → Category A
2
Calculate CV
CV = 12 ÷ 30 = 0.40 → below 0.5 → Category X
3
Final classification
Class AX → Fully automated replenishment. No manual review needed.
2

Exponential Smoothing Forecast

Weight recent sales more heavily — auto-detect trends without manual judgment

How It Works
St = α × Actualt-1 + (1−α) × St-1
α = 0.3
30% weight on today's actual sales
Weight Decay
Today 30% → Yesterday 21% → 2 days ago 14.7% → decreasing...
Why Not Simple Average?
A spike last month shouldn't weigh the same as yesterday. Exponential smoothing self-adjusts.
📋 Example — Forecasting Day 4
1
Day 1
Actual = 50 → S₁ = 50
2
Day 2
Actual = 60 → S₂ = 0.3×60 + 0.7×50 = 53
3
Day 3
Actual = 45 → S₃ = 0.3×45 + 0.7×53 = 50.6
4
Day 4 Forecast
Use S₃ ≈ 51 units — blends trend without overreacting to Day 3 dip
3

Sigmoid Continuous Correction

Scale orders up or down smoothly — no rigid thresholds or sudden jumps

The Formula
Adj = 0.2 + 1.1 ÷ (1 + EXP(−4 × (STR − 1)))
STR (Sales Boost Rate)
Actual sales ÷ Expected sales. 0.8 = selling at 80% of target.
Output Range
~0.2 (slow sales) → ~1.3 (booming sales)
Why Sigmoid?
STR 79%→81% transitions coefficient 0.78→0.82 smoothly, not a cliff-jump
📋 Example — Sluggish Sales Week
1
Expected
100 units forecasted
2
Actual sold
80 units → STR = 0.80
3
Calculate Adj
0.2 + 1.1÷(1+EXP(−4×(0.8−1))) = ~0.54
4
Effect
Thursday order reduced to 54% of base — gentle pullback without overreaction
4

Dual-Track Delivery System

Two orders per week: one cautious, one corrective — risk window cut from 7 → 3.5 days

The Two Orders
Monday (Order 1) — Conservative
= Forecast × 3.5 days × 0.8
0.8 buffer prevents overstocking if forecast is too high
Thursday (Order 2) — Flexible
= Forecast × 3.5 days × Adj × MidAdj
Corrects Monday's estimate using mid-week actual data
Why It Matters
Forecast errors corrected in 3.5 days — not locked in for a full 7-day cycle
📋 Example — "Greek Yogurt 500g"
1
Daily Forecast
Smoothed = 20 units/day
2
Monday Order
20 × 3.5 × 0.8 = 56 units (conservative)
3
Mid-Week Reality
Mon–Wed actual = 75 units → STR = 1.07 (above forecast!)
4
Thursday Order
20 × 3.5 × 1.05 ≈ 73 units — detects spike, replenishes aggressively
5

Three-Level Circuit Breaker

Automatic escalation to stop replenishing dead stock

Escalation Levels
👁
Level 1 — Watch
1 week sell-through < 30% → Flagged for observation. No action yet.
🔒
Level 2 — Cap
2 consecutive weeks < 40% → Orders hard-capped at ≤ 50 units.
🚫
Level 3 — Suspend
3 consecutive weeks < 30% → ALL replenishment halted. Audit required.
📋 Example — "Detox Tea"
1
Week 1
Sell-through = 25% → Level 1: flagged
2
Week 2
Sell-through = 35% → Level 2: capped ≤ 50 units
3
Week 3
Sell-through = 22% → Level 3: SUSPENDED
4
Next Step
Team investigates root cause. Replenishment resumes only after resolution.
